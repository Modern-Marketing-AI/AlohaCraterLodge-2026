The Silence Before Episode 42: Forecast Window Feb 11-16

The summit of Kilauea has gone quiet. Too quiet.

For the past 48 hours, seismicity at the caldera has dropped significantly, yet ground deformation monitors (tiltmeters) are showing a sharp inflationary spike. In plain English: The balloon is filling up, but it hasn't popped yet.

What This Means for Visitors

Historically, this specific pattern—rapid inflation coupled with a drop in earthquakes—precedes a surface breakout by 24 to 72 hours. Our local contacts at the observatory are monitoring a intrusion directly beneath the Halema'uma'u floor.

If you are arriving between February 11th and 16th, keep your cameras ready. We are likely entering the "Episode 42" phase of this eruption cycle.

Viewing Strategy

We recommend heading to the devastation trail overlook immediately after sunset. The glow is currently visible from the lodge lanai, but a breakout will illuminate the entire cloud deck.
